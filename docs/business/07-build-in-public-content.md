# Build-in-Public Video & Content Guide

> For Instagram Reels / TikTok — a solo developer who trains calisthenics and built the app for himself.
> You are NOT a marketer. You are NOT a filmmaker. You are a developer who trains. That IS the content.

---

## Part 1: The Fundamentals (Read This First)

### Why People Follow Fitness Accounts

People don't follow apps. People follow **people**. They follow someone they want to be like, learn from, or root for. Your account works because you sit at an unusual intersection:

```
Developer who codes at night
        +
Calisthenics athlete who trains at the park
        +
Solo founder building something from nothing
```

Almost nobody is in this intersection. That's your unfair advantage. You're not competing against fitness influencers with 6-packs and ring lights. You're the underdog building his own thing — and the internet loves that story.

### The 3 Emotions That Make People Follow

Every video should trigger at least one:

| Emotion | What Triggers It | Example |
|---------|-----------------|---------|
| **Curiosity** | Something unexpected, a question unanswered | "I built AI that counts your macros from a photo" |
| **Aspiration** | Seeing a result they want for themselves | Your progress photos, your consistency, your app working |
| **Connection** | Feeling like they know you, your struggle is theirs | Late night coding, failing a muscle-up, honest moments |

### The Universal Video Structure

Every short-form video that works follows this pattern:

```
HOOK (0-3 seconds)
  ↓ They stop scrolling
TENSION (3-10 seconds)
  ↓ They want to see what happens
PAYOFF (10-20 seconds)
  ↓ They got the value / surprise / answer
CTA (last 2-3 seconds)
  ↓ They know what to do next
```

That's it. Every video. Learn this structure and you can make anything work.

## Part 2: How to Actually Make a Video

### Equipment You Need

| What | What to Use | Cost |
|------|-------------|------|
| Camera | Your phone (front camera for face, back camera for exercises) | Free |
| Tripod | A cheap phone tripod or lean your phone against a water bottle | $10-15 |
| Audio | Your phone's mic is fine. Or use earbuds/AirPods for voiceover | Free |
| Editing | CapCut (free app, best for Reels/TikTok) | Free |
| Captions | CapCut auto-captions (fix the errors manually) | Free |
| Screen recording | iOS built-in screen recorder / Android built-in | Free |

### The 4 Types of Shots You'll Use

**1. Talking Head (Tu cara hablando a camara)**
- Hold your phone at eye level, front camera
- Look at the camera lens, not the screen
- Good lighting = face a window during the day, or any lamp at night
- This is for hooks and explanations
- Frame: head and shoulders, a little space above your head

**2. Screen Recording (La pantalla de tu app)**
- Use your phone's built-in screen recorder
- Clean up notifications first (Do Not Disturb mode)
- Tap slowly and deliberately — fast taps look messy on video
- Zoom into important parts in editing (CapCut pinch-to-zoom)
- This is for showing the app, the code, the features

**3. Exercise Footage (Tu entrenando)**
- Set up your phone on a tripod at the park or wherever you train
- Film from the side for most exercises (shows the full movement)
- Film from the front for pull-ups, dips (shows effort on your face)
- Natural light outdoors is always better than indoors
- This is for training content and showing you're real

**4. B-Roll / Ambient (Tomas de ambiente)**
- Walking to the park, your laptop screen, your hands typing code, the park bars
- These fill gaps between talking head and main content
- Film a bunch of these in one session and reuse them across videos
- 2-3 second clips, slightly moving camera

### How to Film a Batch Session (Sunday, 2-3 hours)

```
Hour 1: At home
  - Film 3-4 talking head hooks (different shirts if possible)
  - Film 2-3 screen recordings of app features
  - Film your code editor / terminal (for dev content)

Hour 2: At the park
  - Film 5-6 exercises from your program
  - Film the app open next to the bar (phone on ground showing today's workout)
  - Film yourself completing a workout and tapping "share" on the app
  - Film ambient shots: the park, the bars, walking there

Hour 3: Editing (at home)
  - Open CapCut, combine clips into 3-5 Reels
  - Add text overlays, captions, music
  - Export and schedule for the week
```

This gives you 3-5 Reels from one session. You're done for the week.

### Editing in CapCut (The Only Things You Need to Know)

1. **Start with the hook** — put your most attention-grabbing clip or text first
2. **Cut dead air** — trim any pause longer than 0.5 seconds. Speed = retention.
3. **Add text overlays** — big text that summarizes what you're saying/showing. Most people watch on mute.
4. **Add auto-captions** — CapCut generates them. Fix spelling errors.
5. **Add music** — pick trending audio from Instagram's library or CapCut's. Keep it low volume under voiceover.
6. **Keep it under 30 seconds** — until you have 1K+ followers. Short = more views.

## Part 3: Video Blueprints

Each blueprint below tells you exactly what to film, in what order, and what text/message to use. Pick one, film it, post it.

### Blueprint A: "The Dev Who Trains"

**Category:** Identity / Connection
**Goal:** People follow you because they relate to the crossover
**Length:** 15-20 seconds
**Frequency:** 1 per week

```
SHOT 1 (0-2s): HOOK
  Visual: You at your laptop, code on screen
  Text overlay: "POV: Developer que tambien hace calistenia"
  Audio: Trending sound or lo-fi beat

SHOT 2 (2-5s): TRANSITION
  Visual: Close-up of your hand closing the laptop
  OR: You standing up from the desk

SHOT 3 (5-12s): THE CONTRAST
  Visual: You at the park doing pull-ups / muscle-ups / dips
  Text overlay: "El unico gym que necesito"
  Cut between 2-3 exercises, 2 seconds each

SHOT 4 (12-18s): THE APP
  Visual: Your phone showing the app — workout complete screen with confetti
  Text overlay: "Y la app que lo trackea? La hice yo."

SHOT 5 (18-20s): CTA
  Text overlay: "Link en bio — es gratis"
```

**Message:** "I live in both worlds — code and calisthenics. That makes me different."
**Why it works:** Nobody else is in this intersection. It's memorable.

### Blueprint B: "The Feature Demo"

**Category:** Product / Curiosity
**Goal:** People see the app doing something impressive and want to try it
**Length:** 15-25 seconds
**Frequency:** 1 per week

```
SHOT 1 (0-3s): HOOK
  Visual: Screen recording of the AI nutrition feature
  Text overlay: "Una IA que cuenta tus macros en 2 segundos"
  OR: Your face saying this to camera

SHOT 2 (3-12s): THE DEMO
  Visual: Screen recording showing the full flow
    - Type "2 huevos con tostada y cafe con leche"
    - AI returns: 380 kcal, 24g protein, 28g carbs, 18g fat
    - Show the macros bar filling up
  Text overlay: "Sin escanear codigos. Solo escribe."
  Film this SLOWLY — people need to read the screen

SHOT 3 (12-18s): THE CONTEXT
  Visual: You talking to camera or text overlay
  Text: "Llevo 3 meses trackeando mi nutricion asi.
         La app es gratis y la hice yo."

SHOT 4 (18-22s): CTA
  Text overlay: "Link en bio"
  OR: "Comenta 'APP' y te mando el link"
```

**Message:** "This specific feature is impressive. And it's free. And I built it."
**Why it works:** AI nutrition tracking is your most demo-able, unique feature. It looks like magic on screen.

### Blueprint C: "The Progress Story"

**Category:** Aspiration / Social Proof
**Goal:** People see real results and think "I want that"
**Length:** 20-30 seconds
**Frequency:** 2 per month

```
SHOT 1 (0-3s): HOOK
  Visual: Side-by-side progress photo (or app progress chart)
  Text overlay: "12 semanas de calistenia"
  Audio: Motivational or trending sound

SHOT 2 (3-10s): THE JOURNEY
  Visual: Quick montage of training clips (2 sec each)
    - Early exercises (struggling)
    - Recent exercises (stronger)
    - OR: Scroll through the app's calendar heatmap showing consistency
  Text overlay: "Sin gimnasio. Sin pesas. Solo mi cuerpo."

SHOT 3 (10-18s): THE DATA
  Visual: Screen recording of the app
    - Show progress charts
    - Show streak count
    - Show session history
  Text overlay: "Todo registrado en mi app"

SHOT 4 (18-25s): THE EMOTION
  Visual: You at the park, sunset, or a quiet moment
  Text overlay: "No se trata de ser perfecto.
                 Se trata de no parar."

SHOT 5 (25-28s): CTA
  Text: "La app es gratis. Link en bio."
```

**Message:** "Real progress happens with consistency. Here's proof. Here's the tool."
**Why it works:** Transformation content is the #1 performing fitness content type. Your app makes the story measurable.

### Blueprint D: "The Builder Story"

**Category:** Connection / Build-in-Public
**Goal:** People root for you as the underdog. Developer audience + fitness audience overlap.
**Length:** 30-45 seconds (longer format, fewer per month)
**Frequency:** 2 per month

```
SHOT 1 (0-3s): HOOK
  Visual: Your GitHub commit graph or terminal
  Text overlay: "Llevo 6 meses construyendo una app de calistenia. Solo."
  Audio: Calm/emotional trending sound

SHOT 2 (3-10s): THE BEGINNING
  Visual: Screenshot of Notes app with a messy workout list
  Text overlay: "Asi empeze. Apuntando mis rutinas en Notas."
  Transition to: Early version of the app (if you have screenshots)

SHOT 3 (10-20s): THE BUILD
  Visual: Quick montage:
    - Code editor with your code
    - Terminal running the build
    - The app on your phone, evolving
    - Maybe a commit message that's fun or late-night
  Text overlay: "200+ archivos. 41 paginas. 101 migraciones de base de datos."

SHOT 4 (20-30s): THE NOW
  Visual: Screen recording of the app today — polished, working, beautiful
    - Show 2-3 features quickly: workout tracking, share card, AI nutrition
  Text overlay: "Hoy es una app completa. Y es gratis."

SHOT 5 (30-40s): THE WHY
  Visual: You talking to camera, genuine
  Text/voiceover: "La hice porque ninguna app hacia lo que yo necesitaba.
                   Ahora la usan mis amigos. Y quiero que la uses tu."

SHOT 6 (40-45s): CTA
  Text overlay: "gym.guille.tech — link en bio"
```

**Message:** "I built this thing from nothing, for myself, and now I'm sharing it with you."
**Why it works:** People love origin stories. This makes them emotionally invested in your success.

### Blueprint E: "The Training Tip"

**Category:** Value / Education
**Goal:** People learn something useful. They follow for more tips. The app is secondary.
**Length:** 15-25 seconds
**Frequency:** 1 per week

```
SHOT 1 (0-3s): HOOK
  Visual: You doing the exercise wrong (intentionally)
  Text overlay: "El error #1 en las dominadas"
  OR: "3 progresiones para tu primera dominada"

SHOT 2 (3-15s): THE VALUE
  Visual: Exercise demonstration
    - Show the wrong way (X overlay or red tint)
    - Show the right way (check mark or green tint)
    - OR: Show 3 progressions, 3 seconds each
  Text overlay: Brief cue for each ("Activa la escapula", "Baja controlado", etc.)

SHOT 3 (15-20s): THE SOFT SELL
  Visual: Your phone showing the app with the exercise programmed
  Text overlay: "Este ejercicio esta en mi programa gratuito"
  OR: Just leave the app visible on screen — don't even mention it

SHOT 4 (20-23s): CTA
  Text: "Guardalo para tu proximo entreno"
  OR: "Sigueme para mas tips"
```

**Message:** "Here's genuinely useful fitness advice. Oh, and I have an app that organizes all of this for you."
**Why it works:** Value-first content builds trust. The app sells itself by being visible, not by being pitched.

### Blueprint F: "The Challenge"

**Category:** Engagement / Virality
**Goal:** People participate, tag friends, create UGC
**Length:** 15-20 seconds
**Frequency:** 1 per month

```
SHOT 1 (0-3s): HOOK
  Visual: You doing the challenge exercise
  Text overlay: "Reto: 50 flexiones en 2 minutos"
  OR: "Puedes hacer 10 dominadas seguidas?"

SHOT 2 (3-12s): YOU DOING IT
  Visual: Film yourself attempting the challenge
    - Show the struggle, the effort, the real count
    - Show the app timer running
  Text overlay: Show your count in real time

SHOT 3 (12-17s): THE RESULT
  Visual: App completion screen or your exhausted face
  Text overlay: Your result: "47/50 — casi!"

SHOT 4 (17-20s): THE CHALLENGE
  Text overlay: "Tu turno. Grabalo y etiquetame."
  OR: "Reta a tu amigo que dice que entrena"
```

**Message:** "Can you beat this? Prove it."
**Why it works:** Challenges create engagement, tags, and UGC. Your app's challenge feature is the natural next step.

## Part 4: The Messaging Framework

### What You're Selling (Without Selling)

You never say "download my app." Instead, you embody these messages:

| Message | How You Show It | Never Say |
|---------|----------------|-----------|
| "Consistency beats intensity" | Your streak, your heatmap, your daily training | "Track your consistency with my app" |
| "You don't need a gym" | You training at a park, at home, anywhere | "Replace your gym membership" |
| "Free doesn't mean bad" | The app being beautiful and feature-rich | "It's free unlike other apps" |
| "I built this for myself" | Showing your own data, your own progress | "I built this for users like you" |
| "Training is better with friends" | Showing the challenge/leaderboard features | "Invite your friends to compete" |

### The CTA Rotation

Don't use the same CTA every video. Rotate:

| CTA | When to Use | Goal |
|-----|------------|------|
| "Link en bio" | Feature demos, builder stories | Drive signups |
| "Sigueme para mas" | Training tips, first few videos | Grow followers |
| "Guardalo para despues" | Exercise tutorials, tips | Algorithm boost (saves = reach) |
| "Comenta 'APP' y te lo mando" | Feature demos | DM conversations + engagement |
| "Etiqueta a alguien que entrena" | Challenges, motivation | Reach new people |
| "Tu turno — grabalo" | Challenges | UGC creation |
| No CTA at all | When the content is strong enough | Sometimes no CTA performs better |

### Caption Writing (The Text Below the Video)

Keep it short. 2-3 sentences max. Structure:

```
Line 1: Expand on the video slightly (not repeat it)
Line 2: Personal reflection or question
Line 3: CTA

Example:
"Llevo 12 semanas entrenando sin fallar un solo dia.
La app me ayuda a no romper la racha — funciona de verdad.
Link en bio si quieres probarla."
```

## Part 5: Growth Tactics

### The First 30 Days (0 to 500 Followers)

| Week | What to Post | What to Do |
|------|-------------|------------|
| 1 | 3 videos: 1 dev crossover + 1 feature demo + 1 training tip | Follow 50 calisthenics accounts, like and comment genuinely |
| 2 | 3 videos: 1 builder story + 1 feature demo + 1 challenge | Reply to every comment on your videos. DM everyone who likes. |
| 3 | 3 videos: 1 progress story + 1 training tip + 1 dev crossover | Collaborate with 1 small creator (film together at the park) |
| 4 | 3 videos: 1 builder story + 1 feature demo + 1 training tip | Ask followers to share. Repost anyone who shares their workout card. |

### What Makes the Algorithm Push Your Video

| Signal | What It Means | How to Optimize |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Watch time** | People watch till the end | Keep it short (15-20s). Hook in first 2 seconds. |
| **Saves** | People save it for later | Make it useful (tutorials, tips, exercises to try) |
| **Shares** | People send it to friends | Make it relatable ("tag someone who...") or challengeable |
| **Comments** | People write something | Ask a question. Be controversial. Challenge them. |
| **Replays** | People watch it again | Fast editing, satisfying visuals, dense information |

### The Content That Gets Shared vs. Followed

| Gets SHARED (reach) | Gets FOLLOWED (loyalty) |
|---------------------|------------------------|
| Challenges ("can you do this?") | Builder stories (your journey) |
| Relatable moments ("when your friend says he trains...") | Training tips (ongoing value) |
| Impressive demos (AI feature, progress photos) | Personal progress (people want updates) |

You need both. Shared content brings new people. Follow-worthy content makes them stay.

## Part 6: Languages

### Bilingual Strategy

Your app is bilingual. Your content can be too:

| Language | Platform | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| **Spanish** (primary) | Instagram, TikTok | Your core audience. Lower competition. LATAM market is massive. |
| **English** (secondary) | TikTok, Reddit | Larger total audience. Dev/build-in-public community is English-first. |

**Practical approach:**
- Film once, edit twice (Spanish captions + English captions)
- OR: Post in Spanish on Instagram, English on TikTok
- Code-related content (builder stories) works in English naturally
- Training content works in Spanish naturally

## Part 7: What NOT to Do

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Don't start with "Hey guys, today I want to show you..." | Instant scroll. Start with the hook, not a greeting. |
| Don't film horizontal video | Reels/TikTok is 9:16 vertical. Horizontal = cropped and ugly. |
| Don't make videos longer than 30 seconds (at first) | Shorter = higher completion rate = more reach. Earn the right to go longer. |
| Don't post without captions/text | 80%+ watch on mute. No text = nobody knows what's happening. |
| Don't ask "What do you think?" as your CTA | Too vague. Be specific: "Could you do 10 pull-ups in a row?" |
| Don't post and disappear | Reply to every comment for the first hour. The algorithm rewards it. |
| Don't wait for perfect | A shaky phone video with good content beats a polished video with nothing to say. |
| Don't talk about features | Talk about outcomes. Not "we have streak tracking" but "45 dias sin fallar." |
| Don't sell the app | Sell the identity. "Be someone who trains seriously." The app is just the tool. |

```

  □ Plan 3 videos for the week (pick from blueprints)
  □ Film all talking head hooks at home
  □ Film screen recordings of app features
  □ Go to the park — film exercises + ambient shots
  □ Edit 3 Reels in CapCut
  □ Write captions
  □ Schedule Monday, Wednesday, Friday posts

MONDAY / WEDNESDAY / FRIDAY (10 min each):
  □ Post the scheduled Reel
  □ Reply to every comment for 30-60 min after posting

  □ Check DMs, reply to anyone who messaged
```
